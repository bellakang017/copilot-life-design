Why Use an AI Copilot?

Career reflection is personal. A copilot makes the process easier.

Simple

Paste a job posting, get structured analysis. No spreadsheets, no guesswork. The prompt does the thinking for you.

Fast

What normally takes hours of self-analysis happens in a 10-minute conversation. Try it with multiple postings in one sitting.

Emotional Process

Job searching feels overwhelming. This framework helps you focus on energy and fit — not just "Am I qualified?" but "Would I thrive here?"

The 5 Stages

The system prompt guides Copilot through all 5 stages automatically. Here's what each one does.

1

Parse the Posting

Breaks the posting into what the team actually wants — responsibilities, culture signals, growth potential.

2

Mirror Your Profile

Maps your experience — strong matches, gaps, and hidden strengths you might not see.

3

Energy Check

Rates each task on energy and meaning. Would you thrive here — or just survive?

4

Gap to Prototype

Turns skill gaps into 2-week experiments you can start now. Small tests, not big commitments.

5

Craft Your Story

Your narrative: why this role, why you, why now.

[System prompt pasted] Here's the job posting I want to analyze:

"Instructional Designer — UT Austin School of Education. Collaborate with faculty to design innovative online courses..."

Stage 1 — Parse the Posting

Core Responsibilities

  • 1. Design online courses with faculty (collaborative, not solo)
  • 2. Manage course development timelines (project management is central)
  • 3. Evaluate and improve existing course materials

Culture Signals

"Innovative" + "collaborate with faculty" suggests this team values creative autonomy within a partnership model.

Growth Indicators

"School of Education" context = access to research communities and potential to publish on your design work.

Ready for Stage 2? Share your resume or key experiences.

I'm an MA student in Communication. I've TAed for 2 semesters, managed a student org newsletter, and have basic Canva/Google Sites experience. No formal LMS training.

Stage 2 — Mirror Your Profile

STRONG

Faculty collaboration — your TA experience shows you already work within academic teams and understand course structures.

PARTIAL

Project management — newsletter management shows organizational skills, but you'd need to frame it as timeline-driven content production.

GAP

LMS platforms — no Canvas/Blackboard/Moodle experience. This is learnable but listed as required.

HIDDEN

International perspective — your cross-cultural communication lens is valuable for designing inclusive online courses.

Ready for Stage 3? Let's check how each responsibility would feel day-to-day.

Stage 3 — Energy Check

Based on your responses, here's your energy map:

Design courses with faculty

High Energy

Manage development timelines

Neutral

Evaluate existing materials

High Energy

2 of 3 core tasks are energizing. This looks like a good fit on the energy dimension.

Ready for Stage 4? Let's turn your gaps into experiments.

Stage 4 — Gap to Prototype

Your top gap: LMS experience. Here's a 2-week experiment:

Question to test

"Do I enjoy building structured learning experiences in a digital platform?"

2-week experiment

Take the free Canvas LMS basics course (Instructure). Redesign one module from a class you've TAed — apply what you learn.

Who to talk to

Ask your department's instructional support team for a 15-min coffee chat about their daily workflow.

Ready for Stage 5? Let's craft your story.

Stage 5 — Craft Your Story

Opening hook

"As someone who's navigated education systems in two countries, I know that how you design a learning experience shapes who feels included in it."

Evidence bridge

Your TA experience + newsletter management + cross-cultural lens → you don't just design courses, you design them with empathy for diverse learners.

Growth narrative

The LMS gap becomes a strength: "I'm already completing Canvas certification because I want to bring evidence-based design to every module I build."

Closing vision

"I want to help UT's School of Education create online courses that feel as human as being in the room."
