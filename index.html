[System prompt pasted] Here's the job posting:

"Instructional Designer — UT Austin School of Education..."

Stage 1 — Parse the Posting

Core Responsibilities

  • 1. Design online courses with faculty
  • 2. Manage course development timelines
  • 3. Evaluate and improve existing materials

Culture Signals

"Innovative" + "collaborate" = creative autonomy within a partnership model.

Growth Indicators

School of Education = access to research communities, potential to publish.

I'm an MA student in Communication. I've TAed for 2 semesters, managed a student org newsletter, basic Canva experience. No LMS training.

Stage 2 — Mirror Your Profile

STRONG

Faculty collaboration — TA experience shows you work within academic teams.

PARTIAL

Project management — newsletter = organizational skills, needs reframing.

GAP

LMS platforms — no Canvas/Blackboard experience. Learnable but required.

HIDDEN

International perspective — cross-cultural lens valuable for inclusive course design.

Stage 3 — Energy Check

Design courses with faculty

High Energy

Manage development timelines

Neutral

Evaluate existing materials

High Energy

2 of 3 core tasks are energizing. Good fit on the energy dimension.

Stage 4 — Gap to Prototype

Top gap: LMS experience. Here's a 2-week experiment:

Question to test

"Do I enjoy building learning experiences in a digital platform?"

2-week experiment

Free Canvas LMS course + redesign one module from a class you've TAed.

Who to talk to

15-min coffee chat with your department's instructional support team.

Stage 5 — Craft Your Story

Opening hook

"As someone who's navigated education systems in two countries, I know that how you design a learning experience shapes who feels included."

Evidence bridge

TA experience + newsletter + cross-cultural lens = courses designed with empathy.

Growth narrative

LMS gap → strength: "I'm completing Canvas certification to bring evidence-based design to every module."

Closing vision

"I want to create online courses that feel as human as being in the room."
